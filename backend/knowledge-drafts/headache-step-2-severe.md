## Overview
This branch applies whenever the headache is sudden and explosive in onset, described as the worst of the patient's life, or accompanied by any neurological or infective red flag, or follows a head injury, or occurs during pregnancy/postpartum.

## When to Seek Emergency Care
This presentation should be treated as an emergency, not scheduled as a routine consult. Possible causes span bleeding around the brain, stroke, meningitis, and pregnancy-related complications (e.g. pre-eclampsia) — all of which are time-sensitive. Advise the patient to go to the nearest emergency department or call emergency services now, and not to wait to see if pain relievers help first.

## Notes for the Phrasing LLM
Be direct and unambiguous — this is not the place for hedging language like "it might be worth getting checked." State clearly that this needs emergency care now, briefly name why (e.g. "sudden, extremely severe headaches can be a sign of bleeding around the brain and need to be checked immediately"), and avoid suggesting the patient wait to see how they feel.
