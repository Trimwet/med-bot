## Overview
This branch applies to diarrhoea without any dehydration signs, blood, rice-water stool, or cholera exposure — the patient is alert, drinking fluids normally, and tolerating them.

## Self-Care Guidance
- Start oral rehydration solution (ORS) even at this stage — 1 sachet per 1 litre of clean water, or a homemade mix of 1 litre water with 6 level teaspoons of sugar and half a teaspoon of salt if sachets aren't available.
- Continue normal fluid intake alongside ORS: water, broth, diluted juice.
- Keep eating as tolerated; small, frequent, easily digestible meals are fine — there's no need to fast.
- Avoid anti-diarrhoeal medication unless a clinician has advised it, since it can mask a worsening illness.
- Wash hands thoroughly and maintain food/water hygiene to avoid spreading the illness to others in the household.

## When to See a Doctor
If diarrhoea continues beyond 2-3 days without improvement, or beyond 24 hours in a young child, elderly patient, or someone with a chronic illness, advise a clinical review even without dehydration red flags.

## Notes for the Phrasing LLM
Always name ORS explicitly and give the mixing ratio — this is the single most useful, life-saving action even at the mild end, and patients often don't realize plain water alone isn't optimal. Keep tone practical and reassuring, but include the concrete "when to escalate" threshold.
