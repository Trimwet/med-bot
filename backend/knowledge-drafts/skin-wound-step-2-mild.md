## Overview
This branch applies to a small wound or localized rash with no rapid spreading, red streaking, fever, severe pain, discoloration, or high-risk factors (diabetes, poor circulation, weakened immune system) — consistent with a minor cut, scrape, insect bite, or mild irritant rash.

## Self-Care Guidance
- Clean the wound gently with soap and clean water; avoid harsh antiseptics that can irritate healthy skin.
- Cover with a clean dressing and change it daily, or sooner if it becomes wet or dirty.
- Keep the area dry between dressing changes and avoid picking at scabs.
- For rashes, avoid new soaps, lotions, or fabrics that may be irritants until it settles, and avoid scratching to reduce infection risk.
- A cool compress can ease itching or mild discomfort.

## When to See a Doctor
If the wound isn't visibly improving by day 2-3, if a rash is spreading slowly, persists beyond a week, or keeps recurring, advise the patient to see a doctor.
