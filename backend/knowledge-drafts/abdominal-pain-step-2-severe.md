## Overview
This branch applies whenever the abdominal pain is sudden and severe in onset, accompanied by fever, persistent vomiting, GI bleeding, pregnancy-related bleeding/dizziness, radiating pain, a rigid/distended abdomen, or jaundice. Possible causes span appendicitis, ectopic pregnancy, perforated ulcer, ruptured aneurysm, and other surgical emergencies — all time-sensitive.

## When to Seek Emergency Care
This presentation should be treated as an emergency, not scheduled as a routine consult. Advise the patient to go to the nearest emergency department immediately, or call emergency services if the pain is incapacitating or the patient cannot travel safely on their own. Do not give food or drink if surgery may be needed, and do not delay travel to try pain relief first.

## Notes for the Phrasing LLM
Be direct and unambiguous — this is not the place for hedging language like "it might be worth getting checked." State clearly that this needs emergency care now, and if pregnancy-related bleeding is mentioned, name ectopic pregnancy risk plainly so the patient understands the urgency without unnecessary alarm.
