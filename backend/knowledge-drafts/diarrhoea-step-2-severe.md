## Overview
This branch applies whenever any dehydration sign, inability to tolerate fluids, altered mental state, blood in stool, rice-water stool, or cholera exposure is present. Severe dehydration from acute watery diarrhoea can progress to shock and death within hours without treatment, so this is treated as a time-sensitive emergency rather than a routine consult.

## When to Seek Emergency Care
Advise the patient to go to the nearest health facility, hospital, or cholera treatment centre immediately. If the patient can still drink, encourage sips of oral rehydration solution (ORS) on the way, but do not let this delay seeking emergency care — IV fluids are often needed at this stage and cannot be given at home. If confusion, drowsiness, or inability to sit up unaided is present, the patient should be accompanied and taken in urgently rather than sent alone.

## Notes for the Phrasing LLM
Be direct and unambiguous — this is not the place for hedging language like "monitor and see." State clearly that this needs urgent facility-level care now, briefly explain why (severe dehydration can lead to shock within hours), and avoid suggesting the patient wait to see if home fluids are enough. If cholera exposure or rice-water stool is mentioned, note this explicitly to the patient as a reason for urgency without causing panic.
