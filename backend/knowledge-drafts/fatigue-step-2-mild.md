## Overview
This branch applies to generalized tiredness without one-sided weakness, sickle cell crisis pattern, breathing difficulty, pallor, jaundice, or fever in someone with a chronic illness — consistent with common causes like poor sleep, stress, or a minor short-lived illness.

## Self-Care Guidance
- Prioritize consistent sleep and rest.
- Stay hydrated and eat regular, balanced meals — skipped meals are a common contributor to low energy.
- Consider recent stressors, workload, or illness that might explain the tiredness.
- Light physical activity (e.g. a short walk) can sometimes help more than complete rest, if tolerated.

## When to See a Doctor
If fatigue persists beyond a week without a clear explanation, is getting worse, or starts affecting work, sleep, or daily activities, advise the patient to see a doctor — basic blood tests can rule out anemia, thyroid problems, or other underlying causes.

Keep this reassuring but not dismissive — end with a clear threshold (a week / worsening / new symptoms) so the patient knows what should prompt them to seek care.
