## Overview
This branch applies to a short-duration cough with no fever, no breathing difficulty, no blood in the cough, and no weight loss/night sweats — consistent with a common cold or mild upper respiratory infection.

## Self-Care Guidance
- Rest and stay well hydrated.
- Warm fluids (tea, warm water with honey) can soothe throat irritation.
- Avoid smoke, dust, and other airway irritants where possible.
- Over-the-counter cough remedies may be used per label instructions if the patient has no medical reason to avoid them.

## When to See a Doctor
If the cough persists beyond 2-3 weeks, develops a fever, or starts to feel worse rather than better, advise the patient to seek a clinical review — a cough lasting this long should not simply be assumed to be a cold.

## Notes for the Phrasing LLM
Be reassuring for the short-term, self-limiting case, but be specific about the 2-3 week threshold as the concrete signal to escalate — don't leave "if it doesn't get better" vague.
