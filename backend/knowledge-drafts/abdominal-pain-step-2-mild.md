## Overview
This branch applies to abdominal pain that is mild-to-moderate, without sudden/severe onset, fever, persistent vomiting, bleeding, pregnancy concern, radiating pain, or a rigid/distended abdomen — consistent with common causes such as indigestion, gas, mild gastritis, or a minor stomach upset.

## Self-Care Guidance
- Rest and avoid heavy or fatty meals until symptoms settle.
- Sip clear fluids regularly; avoid alcohol.
- Avoid NSAIDs (e.g. ibuprofen, diclofenac) as these can worsen stomach irritation — paracetamol is a gentler option if pain relief is needed.
- A warm compress on the abdomen can help with cramping discomfort.
- Eat bland, easily digestible foods as tolerated once nausea settles.

## When to See a Doctor
If the pain persists beyond 24-48 hours, worsens, recurs frequently, or starts interfering with eating, drinking, sleep, or daily activity, advise the patient to see a doctor rather than continuing to self-manage.
