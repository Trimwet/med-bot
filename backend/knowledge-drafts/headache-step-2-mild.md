## Overview
This branch applies to gradual-onset headaches without fever, stiff neck, neurological symptoms, trauma, or pregnancy-related concern — consistent with a common tension-type headache or uncomplicated migraine.

## Self-Care Guidance
- Rest in a quiet, dimly lit space if light or noise worsens the pain.
- Drink water; dehydration is a common contributor.
- Eat something if a meal was skipped.
- A standard-dose over-the-counter analgesic (e.g. paracetamol) may be appropriate, following label directions, unless the patient has a medical reason to avoid it.
- Note common triggers for future prevention: poor sleep, skipped meals, dehydration, stress, screen time, excess caffeine or caffeine withdrawal.

## When to See a Doctor
If the headache has not improved within 48 hours, is getting worse rather than better, is becoming more frequent, or is starting to interfere with work, sleep, or daily activities, advise the patient to see a doctor rather than continuing to self-manage.

## Notes for the Phrasing LLM
Keep this reassuring but not dismissive — end with a clear, concrete threshold for when to seek care (48 hours / worsening / new symptoms), so the patient knows what "not improving" should trigger.
